Understanding the Purpose of Sampling

In order to study temporal trends in the Spokane River's major tributaries, we need to collect water samples regularly. Regular sampling ensures that we are getting an accurate picture of the water's quality. Water samples are tested for temperature, total phosphorus, alkalinity, pH, and dissolved oxygen. These parameters, among others, can help us understand how the chemistry of a water system changes with time. In the Mathews Lab, we are specifically interested in phosphorus concentrations along with other contaminants that can affect water quality.
